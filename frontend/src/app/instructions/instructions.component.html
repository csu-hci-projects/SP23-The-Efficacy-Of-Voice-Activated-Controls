Welcome!
Purpose:

This experiment is to measure the efficacy and accuracy of voice control versus mouse input.

Contents:

There will be two tests involved with this experiment. The first test will be completed clicking the squares. The second test will be completed using vocal commands through a microphone. The test will be administered by playing the classic game Simon Says.

Game Rules:

There are four buttons arranged in a square. Each button has a different color, number, and tone associated with it. When you are ready, click Start to begin. The game will display a sequence of colors in a specific order. It is your job to memorize and select those colors in whatever order it asks as accurately as possible. After the sequence plays, you press the Record Command button below before inputing commands. After your sequence is entered, the game will notify you just below where the squares are if you got the sequence correct or incorrect. If you are ever confused what phase you are in, prompts are displayed just above the squares in the game. The game will start with one color and increase by 1 with each successful iteration. You have 2 attempts to achieve a high score round of a given sequence. With each incorrect entry, you lose an attempt and you start again with a new sequence (one color). Current round and attempts are displayed below the game. After 2 attempts you will move on to the next portion of the experiment. Which is another instruction screen containing information for the second test.

The first test is performed by mouse input and is shown to the right. After the sequence has finished playing, please click the Record Command button to begin input, and then click the corresponding squares you were shown. Be sure to click firmly so the tone sounds. Once a square is pressed, the command is recorded and there is no going back.

NOTE: the sequence will play immiediately after pressing start. Make sure you're prepared to memorize! You can take your time to process the sequence before pressing record. If you have any questions, please ask the administrator.
